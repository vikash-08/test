Ramdhari Singh Dinkar

1908-1974

National Poet of India

Dinkar ji was born on 24 September 1908 in a Bhumihar Brahmin family in Simaria village of Begusarai district of Bihar . He did his BA in History and Political Science from Patna University . He had studied Sanskrit , Bengali , English and Urdu in depth. After passing the BA examination, he became a teacher in a school. From 1934 to 1947, he worked in the service of the Bihar government on the posts of Sub-Registrar and Deputy Director of Publicity Department. From 1950 to 1952, he was the Head of the Hindi Department in Langat Singh College, Muzaffarpur, worked as the Vice Chancellor of Bhagalpur University from 1963 to 1965 and after that became the Hindi Advisor to the Government of India.

He was also awarded the Padma Vibhushan . He was awarded the Sahitya Akademi Award for his book 'Sanskriti Ke Char Adhyay' [4] and the Bharatiya Jnanpith Award for 'Urvashi '. He will always remain immortal through his writings.

His epic poem Kurukshetra, based on the Mahabharata, a historical event of the Dvapara Yuga , is ranked 74th among the 100 best poems of the world. [5]
